First employee of Worldcoin.

I was part of the team that designed the Worldcoin iris scanning system - the most advanced scanner of its kind.

My responsibilities included deigning a custom camera lens with liquid lens autofocus, managing a team of 7 people and designing the aiming/focus/exposure controller for the imaging unit. The system can image a person's irises in less than 10 seconds from a distance of 20 to 60cm, and track the user's eye for continuous imaging using a gimbal mechanism with optical quality 100x better than the Aadhar project.

The system is now in production and is being used by millions of people on a portable device all over the world.

My Philosophy

10 Minutes

TLDR:

Think about your three core interests. Why do you care about these things?

(1) Understanding the physical and psychological limit of my body and mind is a topic that is fascinating to me. Through expeditions in precare conditions, self experiment, mental challenges, extreme outdoor activities, I want to prove that anything is achievable. Most of what we want to do and what we think we cannot is usually simply a battle between you and your inner voice, learning to control both and make them friend under any conditions is self-empowering and very interesting. (2) Excelling at multiple discipline from various field. From cooking to freestyle skiing, from programming to art, I love learning new disciplines, understanding concepts. It could be anything. I am always hyper pro-active in the way I manage my time and I am constantly updating my goals like an in-catchable moving target. The reason I care about this is that knowledge help me build relationship, it helps me understand humans and forge deeper connection with people from very different fields. (3) I care a lot about what people think and how they feel. I always try to make them confortable, in security and try to understand them as much as I can. I like being a confident and try to devote time helping people I care the most achieve their goals.I am good at talking one to one with people and always try to inspire and motivate. I want to build something that helps human be healthier and more motivated. I have not figured out how yet, but I will. .

What has been the biggest change to your beliefs or values in the past two years, and what caused this change?

I have always pictured knowledge as a dark map with unknown dimensions that you can explore throughout your life. Some areas are hidden behind doors that need to be opened to even know they exist. I have started to be super aware that on top of that map there is a timer that limits my time. The more you learn new disciplines, the more you open doors that hide other world, each of unknown sizes, it expands your horizon. I realised that unless we are able to extend our human life, there won’t be enough time to explore everything - this goes against my childhood naivety. Once knowing that, it is ressource intensive for my brain to always try its best to optimise my trajectory, set of activities and current goals towards some area of the map I want to explore. It forces me to prioritise some areas over others, when I would want to not sacrifice anything and explore everything. Its heart breaking to do a ranking and put myself in a category (of activities, people, ideology) that is defined by my current beliefs and perception of the world which are dependent upon my location on the map. Once discovering a new area, my approach is to rapidly climb the highest peak, get an overview of the area, then, move on or explore some of the details. The more I discover areas in different biomes, the more I level up and can reach the top of the peak faster.

In what way are you looking to grow personally ?

Personally, I want to become a person reliable and that genuinely motivates people and makes them happy and feel good. I want to reach self actualisation on the Maslow’s hierarchy to be in the best state of mind for creativity, building and helping. Once there I will get to focus all my energy on exactly what I want and not on stuff I have to. That does not mean I am waiting to be there to become that person, that defines the mountain I want to climb in the near future. I want to devote more time to the less fortunates, and help save the environment. I want to expand my toolset and sharpen my interdisciplinary skills to better comprehend, relate and support people. I also want to have a healthy work life balance and keep time for myself and keep doing all the sports and activities that I love. Finally, I want and I am working on being the same person with everyone and not play games or act differently depending if it’s my boss, my girlfriend or my mom. Some might find this dangerous but I think its aligned with my person. II try to be honest with everyone and myself and not hide feelings, if you ask I will answer.
